<p><p>The biodiesel fuel market is primarily divided into two types: pure biodiesel and non-pure biodiesel. Pure biodiesel, often referred to as B100, is made entirely from renewable resources like vegetable oils or animal fats, offering high biodegradability and low emissions. Non-pure biodiesel, typically blended with conventional diesel (e.g., B20 contains 20% biodiesel and 80% diesel), is more widely used due to compatibility with existing engines and infrastructure. Both types cater to different preferences in sustainability and performance.</p></p>
